## Idea 0 — PLUGng (ReachNG for Artists) ⭐ PRIORITY

**The concept:** ReachNG but for musicians and creatives. Instead of outreach to businesses, it scans an artist's social media presence, discovers music blogs, playlist curators, event promoters, radio producers, A&Rs, and brand managers — then generates personalised pitches to get the artist's music in front of the right people.

"Plug" = connection in Nigerian slang. Every artist needs plugs. Nobody has automated finding them.

**The pain:** Artists spend months manually DMing blogs, sending emails to curators who never reply, paying middlemen for "plugs" that don't convert. The whole process is manual, expensive, and relationship-dependent. An unknown artist with a great song has no system to get heard.

**What you build:**
- Artist signs up, connects their Spotify/Apple Music/Instagram
- System scrapes music blogs, playlist curators (Spotify editorial + independent), radio producers, A&Rs at labels, event promoters, brand managers who do artist deals
- Scores each target by relevance to the artist's genre, following size, and engagement
- Claude generates a personalised pitch per target referencing their playlist, their blog post, their event — never generic
- Sends via email or Instagram DM at scale
- Tracks opens, replies, playlist adds, bookings

**Who pays:** Independent artists, music managers, record labels (indie), entertainment companies like PLUGng.

**Warm lead:** David Avante (Mercury VI / PLUGng) → Bizzle Osikoya network. This is the first client. Pitch it as a tool PLUGng uses to plug their roster of artists.

**Pricing idea:**
- Artist plan: ₦30,000/mo (individual artist)
- Manager plan: ₦80,000/mo (up to 5 artists)
- Label plan: ₦200,000/mo (unlimited roster)

**Why it wins:**
- No competitor exists for Nigerian/African artists specifically
- The African music industry is exploding globally (Afrobeats, Amapiano) — every artist needs international plugs
- Reuses 80% of ReachNG's codebase — social discovery, message generation, Unipile sending, reply tracking are all already built
- David/PLUGng is the proof of concept and the first distribution channel

**Build note:** Start with the David pitch. Get PLUGng as client #1. Let them validate the product before building everything. The MVP is ReachNG with an "artist" vertical and a curators/blogs discovery layer.

**Priority: HIGHEST after ReachNG is generating revenue.**

## Idea 1 — AI Voice Agent for Nigerian Businesses

**The pain:** Every Lagos business misses calls. Clinics, restaurants, real estate agents, salons — a human can't be on the phone 24/7. No Nigerian-specific AI voice product exists that understands Nigerian English, Pidgin, and code-switching.

**What you build:** An AI that answers calls, qualifies leads, books appointments, and takes orders — speaking like a Lagos person. Plugs into any business phone number.

**Stack:** ElevenLabs (voice) + Twilio (calls) + Claude (intelligence). Fine-tune the voice on Nigerian speech patterns.

**Who pays:** Clinics, restaurants, real estate agents, salons, logistics companies.

**Pricing idea:** ₦50,000/mo per business. 100 clients = ₦5M MRR.

**Why it wins:** No US competitor can replicate the cultural/linguistic fit. Nigerian English + Pidgin + code-switching is a moat.

**Priority: HIGHEST. Bigger than ReachNG.**

## Idea 2 — AI Invoice Collection via WhatsApp

**The pain:** Nigerian businesses are owed billions in unpaid invoices. Nobody wants to make the awkward chase-money call. It's cultural — embarrassing for both sides. Human collectors are expensive and inconsistent.

**What you build:** An AI WhatsApp agent that follows up on outstanding invoices automatically on behalf of the business. Friendly reminder on day 7, firmer on day 14, payment plan offer on day 21. All in the business owner's voice. Escalates to human only when needed.

**Stack:** WhatsApp (Unipile) + Claude + MongoDB (invoice tracking). Could integrate with Wave, Zoho Invoice, QuickBooks.

**Who pays:** Any business that sells on credit — contractors, suppliers, service providers, B2B companies, fintechs.

**Pricing idea:** Percentage of recovered debt (5–10%) OR flat ₦30k/mo subscription.

**Why it wins:** The product literally pays for itself. Every naira recovered is proof of value.

**Priority: HIGH.**

## Idea 3 — AI Tender Discovery for Nigerian SMEs

**The pain:** The Nigerian government and large corporations publish hundreds of tenders weekly. Most SMEs never see them because monitoring tender portals is a full-time job. The same connected contractors win everything — not because they're better, but because they're watching.

**What you build:** An AI that scrapes all Nigerian tender portals (BPP, state procurement portals, NIMASA, NNPC, etc.), matches each opportunity to a subscribed business's profile, sends a WhatsApp alert the moment a relevant tender drops — with a draft expression of interest already written by Claude.

**Stack:** Playwright (scraping) + Claude (matching + proposal drafting) + WhatsApp (Unipile).

**Who pays:** Engineering firms, IT companies, construction companies, consultancies, suppliers.

**Pricing idea:** ₦100,000/mo. Even one won contract pays for years of subscription.

**Why it wins:** Government contracts in Nigeria are worth trillions. The market is enormous and completely underserved by software.

**Priority: HIGH.**

## Idea 4 — Micro-Influencer Outreach Automation

**The pain:** Brands want to work with micro-influencers (5K–100K followers) but the manual DM process is brutal. Find creator → check engagement → write personalised pitch → send → track → follow up. Nobody can do this at scale manually.

**What you build:** ReachNG but for the creator economy. AI discovers creators by niche and location, scrapes their engagement rates, generates a personalised collaboration pitch referencing their recent content, and sends it at scale via Instagram DM or email.

**Stack:** Apify (Instagram scraping) + Claude (pitch generation) + Unipile or direct Instagram API.

**Who pays:** Any brand with a marketing budget — fashion, food, fintech, real estate, FMCG.

**Pricing idea:** ₦150,000/mo or $300/mo. This one is global — any brand in the world is a customer.

**Why it wins:** Global market. Influencer marketing is a $20B+ industry. Nobody has automated the discovery-to-outreach pipeline end-to-end.

**Priority: HIGH. Global upside.**

## Idea 5 — WhatsApp AI for Restaurants & Salons

**The pain:** Every Lagos restaurant and salon takes orders and bookings via WhatsApp manually. A human sits there all day typing "We have jollof and pounded yam, what would you like?" or "Saturday 2pm is available." It doesn't scale. Orders get missed. Customers wait.

**What you build:** An AI agent that owns the business WhatsApp number and handles the full conversation — menu, availability, booking, confirmation, reminder, upsell — with no human in the loop. The owner just sees confirmed orders/bookings in a dashboard.

**Stack:** WhatsApp Business API + Claude + simple booking/order DB.

**Who pays:** Restaurants, salons, spas, clinics, event spaces.

**Pricing idea:** ₦30,000/mo. Lagos has 10,000+ restaurants. 100 clients = ₦3M MRR.

**Why it wins:** Extremely tangible ROI. Owner immediately stops spending 4 hours/day on WhatsApp.

**Priority: MEDIUM-HIGH. High volume, lower price point.**

## Idea 6 — AI Competitor Intelligence Reports

**The pain:** Lagos businesses compete completely blind. No affordable tool exists to monitor what competitors are doing — pricing changes, new offerings, promotions, social activity, staff movements, customer complaints.

**What you build:** An AI that monitors your specified competitors across Google Maps reviews, social media, websites, and job boards — and delivers a weekly intelligence brief straight to your WhatsApp or email. Tracks changes over time, flags anomalies.

**Stack:** Playwright/Apify (scraping) + Claude (analysis + report writing) + WhatsApp delivery.

**Who pays:** Real estate agencies, recruitment firms, restaurants, fintechs, law firms — any business with identifiable competitors.

**Pricing idea:** ₦40,000/mo. Easy sell — "know what your competition is doing before your competition knows."

**Why it wins:** US tools like Crayon/Klue cost $1,000+/mo and are built for enterprise. Nothing exists for Lagos SMEs.

**Priority: MEDIUM. Easy build on top of existing stack.**

## Common Thread Across All 6

Every idea replaces one of these:
1. **Embarrassing conversations** (chasing invoices, cold calls)
2. **Repetitive manual conversations** (WhatsApp orders, call answering)
3. **Information asymmetry** (tender blindness, competitor blindness)
4. **Scale bottlenecks** (influencer outreach, business discovery)

Nigerians specifically will pay to avoid uncomfortable conversations. That's a cultural moat.

The other moat: every US SaaS company builds for US behaviour, US accents, US payment rails. Building for WhatsApp-first culture, Nigerian English, and Naira is a defensible position nobody from Silicon Valley can easily replicate.
