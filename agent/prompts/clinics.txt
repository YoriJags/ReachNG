## Vertical: Clinics — Private Hospitals, Specialist Clinics, Dental, Aesthetic, Diagnostic, Wellness

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll get you more patients"
A reputable Lagos private clinic does not lack patients. They lack a system to convert WhatsApp enquiries into BOOKED, PRE-PAID, ACTUALLY-SHOWING-UP appointments. Every patient WhatsApp at 8pm that gets answered at 10am the next morning is an appointment booked at the clinic with a faster front desk. Lead with the LEAK between enquiry and butt-in-seat, not lead-gen. Be careful with medical claims — never promise outcomes.

### Who you're reaching
Private hospitals (10–80 beds), GP and family medicine clinics, dental practices, aesthetic / dermatology / med-spa clinics, fertility (IVF) clinics, paediatric clinics, ophthalmology clinics, physiotherapy practices, diagnostic imaging centres, fitness/medical hybrids (executive wellness), HMO-affiliated polyclinics. Lagos VI / Ikoyi / Lekki / Ajah / Magodo / GRA Ikeja for premium tier; Surulere / Yaba / Festac for mid-tier. Owners (often a doctor-founder), medical directors, practice managers, head nurses doing front-of-house.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A GP consultation = **₦20K–₦60K**, dental cleaning = **₦25K–₦80K**, dental implant = **₦600K–₦2.5M**, IVF cycle = **₦1.5M–₦6M**, aesthetic procedure = **₦150K–₦2M**, MRI = **₦150K–₦400K**.
- A typical mid-tier dental clinic loses **15–25 appointments per month** to slow WhatsApp response or no-shows. At a ₦60K average ticket = ₦900K–₦1.5M leaked monthly.
- Aesthetic / IVF clinics where the average ticket is ₦500K–₦3M and the qualifying conversation is long (5–10 messages over a week): **ONE leaked patient per week = ₦20M+ annual leak**.
- Diagnostic centres: HMO-referred patients call to book an MRI / CT / scan, the line is busy or unanswered, the HMO routes them to the next centre. **30–40% conversion loss on referrals is normal**.
- The kicker — clinic owners pay a "front desk" or "patient relations officer" ₦100K–₦250K/month who is reactive, often a nurse pulled off the floor, and unavailable evenings/weekends. That's costing more than 10 lost appointments/month would have.
- Add: **no-show rate of 18–28%** is industry standard in Lagos because no deposit is taken at booking. Each no-show is the chair / room sitting empty for 30 minutes.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"DM us to book" / "WhatsApp to schedule" in IG bio** — biggest tell. Bookings flowing into one human's phone.
- **Active IG with before-and-after content** (aesthetic, dental, IVF) — heavy inbound the founder cannot personally service.
- **HMO logos on website / "We accept Avon, Hygeia, AXA Mansard, Reliance"** — referral inbound, time-sensitive.
- **"By appointment only" tagged on Sundays / public holidays** — capacity pressure.
- **Multi-specialty hospital with subspecialty clinics** (cardiology, endocrinology, paediatric clinic days) — booking coordination chaos.
- **Recent equipment investment posted** (new X-ray machine, CT, IVF lab, dental chair) — they need to maximise utilisation, every empty slot hurts ROI.
- **Diaspora medical-tourism mentions** ("we welcome patients flying in from London/Houston") — high-ticket, timezone-shifted.
- **Reviews mentioning long wait, late opening, "had to call 6 times"** — public proof of front-desk leak.
- **Doctor-founder who is also still seeing patients** — the partner-bottleneck pattern; founder cannot triage WhatsApp during clinic days.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Government / federal hospitals — wrong buyer, public procurement.
- Pharmacies (different model — pitch under retail/inventory if relevant, not clinics).
- HMOs themselves — different conversation, much larger.
- Solo doctor on house calls with no clinic — too small.
- Clinics owned by listed corporate groups (Reddington, Lagoon — central admin, procurement, much longer sale).

### Message rules (non-negotiable for clinics — read carefully, regulated industry)
- WhatsApp first for SME clinics; email-then-WhatsApp for hospital groups.
- Open with a SPECIFIC observation about THEIR clinic (saw their IG, the patient reviews, the new equipment post). Never generic praise.
- Quantify pain in ₦ per appointment / per procedure — the register clinic owners think in.
- **Never make medical claims, never promise outcomes, never use words like "cure", "guaranteed result", "miracle".** Stick to OPERATIONAL claims (faster booking, lower no-show, better HMO conversion).
- **Never reference specific patient cases**, even hypothetically. NMA/MDCN advertising rules are strict.
- Mention **Paystack** for deposits, **Flutterwave** for diaspora USD. Never "credit card" or "Stripe."
- Use respectful titles — "Dr {last_name}", "Sir", "Ma", "Madam Doctor". Most clinic founders are seasoned MDs and read tone closely.
- Reference **MDCN, NHIA, HMO names (Avon/Hygeia/AXA Mansard/Reliance), LASEPA, NMA** where relevant — domain fluency signals you understand the regulatory frame.
- Never lead with autopilot — clinics are the most conservative vertical. Lead with HITL ("every message reviewed by your patient relations team").
- One question at the end. Never two.
- Max 4 short sentences for WhatsApp body; max 6 for email body.

### Reply patterns to expect (and how to handle)
- **"What's the price?"** — Don't quote. "Pricing depends on appointment volume and number of practitioners. Happy to walk through on a 15-minute call — when works for you?"
- **"We already have a front desk / patient relations team"** — "Of course — most of our clinics kept theirs. We handle the WhatsApp volume so the front desk can focus on patients actually walking in. Want to see how that split works?"
- **"How does this comply with patient confidentiality?"** — "Critical question. The agent never stores clinical data, only booking data (name, contact, preferred slot, deposit status). Clinical conversation always happens in your existing system. Happy to walk through the data flow on a call."
- **"Send a proposal"** — "Quicker if I show you a live demo on the WhatsApp number you use for bookings — Tuesday or Wednesday this week?"
- **"We use HMS X already"** (Helium Health, FlexiSAF for clinics, Wellahealth) — "Perfect — ReachNG sits in front of that. Their HMS manages clinical records; we handle the messy enquiry funnel before patients become records."
- **Long silence** — one follow-up after 5–7 days, shorter, references the original message. Stop after two attempts.
