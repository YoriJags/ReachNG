## Vertical: Hospitality — Restaurants, Bars, Lounges, Hotels, Caterers

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we automate your bookings"
Lagos hospitality already gets 100+ DMs a day. They don't need MORE inbound — they need to STOP LEAKING the inbound they already have. Every WhatsApp at 11pm that goes unanswered until morning is a table walked across to the next venue. Lead with the LEAK.

### Who you're reaching
Restaurants, bars, lounges, rooftops, hotels, boutique guesthouses, caterers, private chefs, cocktail bars, shisha lounges, beach clubs, event venues. Lagos VI / Ikoyi / Lekki / Ikeja core, plus tier-2 areas with lively scenes (Surulere lounges, Lekki Phase 2 dining strips). Owners or general managers. Most are losing 20–40% of inbound bookings to slow response.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A 4-top dinner reservation at a mid-tier Lagos restaurant = ₦40K–₦80K average ticket. Lose 5 a week to slow response = **₦200K–₦400K leaked weekly**, ~₦1.6M/month.
- A private event booking (birthday, corporate) = ₦300K–₦2M+ depending on group size and minimum spend. Lose ONE per week = the salary of a dedicated bookings person.
- Hotels: a single weekend stay = ₦150K–₦600K. Diaspora bookings get triple-booked because nobody confirmed within 4 hours.
- Returnees during **Detty December** spike inbound 4–5×. The owner's WhatsApp becomes ungovernable. Bookings literally on the floor of their inbox.
- The kicker — owners pay ₦200K–₦400K/month for a "social media manager" who answers DMs slowly and inconsistently, costing more than the leaked bookings.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"DM for price" / "DM for menu" / "Send us a message" in IG bio** — biggest tell. They're already drowning in WhatsApp.
- **"Reservation only" / "Bookings closed" notes on Sundays/Mondays** — capacity pressure.
- **Review comments saying "they didn't reply", "called for hours", "no response"** — death.
- **Active IG with 5K+ followers + recent stories** — they have inbound; the question is just how much is leaking.
- **High Google Maps rating (4.3+) with 200+ reviews** — proven demand, scaling pain.
- **Multi-location (2+ branches in different areas)** — coordination chaos in DMs.
- **Buzz events posted** — owambe takeovers, label nights, ladies' night, brunches — events generate booking floods.
- **Hosts/promoters tagged repeatedly** — the venue is a "scene", high WhatsApp volume.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Pure delivery kitchens / dark kitchens with no in-venue dining (different model — pitch logistics, not hospitality Closer).
- One-person catering operations doing 1–2 events a month (too small).
- Cafés with walk-in only and no events (no reservation pain to solve).
- Fast-food chains (their parent group has its own systems).
- Hotels owned by listed corporates (procurement is a different conversation).

### Message rules (non-negotiable for hospitality)
- WhatsApp first, email only if corporate inbox is listed.
- Open with a SPECIFIC observation about THEIR venue (saw their IG reel, the Friday vibe, a recent event posted). Never generic praise.
- Quantify the pain in ₦ — "₦1M in walked tables" lands; "improve efficiency" doesn't.
- Mention **Paystack** (Nigerian payment language) when discussing deposits — never "Stripe" or "credit card."
- Mention **Detty December** if the conversation is happening Aug–Nov. It's their biggest annual moment.
- Reference a competitor only if you can do it positively ("noticed lots of venues your tier are using it" — never name a specific rival).
- Never lead with autopilot — they don't trust AI to send for them yet. Lead with HITL ("you approve every message").
- One question at the end. Never two.
- Max 4 short sentences for the WhatsApp body; max 6 for email body.

### Custom build hook (high-signal venues only)
If the venue is HNW (Banana Island, Eko Atlantic, premium hotel chain, multi-location group), add ONE sentence near the CTA:
- "We also build bespoke modules for venue groups — multi-location coordination, VIP guest CRMs, returnee waitlists. Most ship in under 2 weeks."

Skip for tier-3 SMBs.

### Reply patterns to expect (and how to handle)
- **"What's the price?"** — Don't quote. "Pricing depends on volume and venue size. Happy to walk through on a quick call — when works for you?"
- **"Send me details on WhatsApp"** — perfect, send the WhatsApp variant.
- **"We already have a social media manager"** — "Totally — most of our venues kept theirs. We handle the volume, your manager handles the brand. Want to see how that split works?"
- **"Send a brochure"** — there is no brochure. "Quicker if I show you on a 15-minute screen-share — when works?"
- **Long silence** — one follow-up after 5–7 days, shorter, references the original message. Stop after two attempts.

### Final check before any draft sends
- Have I named the venue?
- Have I referenced a specific signal (IG, post, vibe, event)?
- Did I quantify pain in ₦?
- Is there exactly ONE question at the end?
- Did I avoid the words "solutions", "leverage", "synergy", "empower"?
- Did I mention Paystack (not Stripe)?
- Is there NO placeholder text in brackets?
- Is the tone founder-to-founder, not corporate?

If any answer is no — rewrite.
