## Vertical: Agriculture — Commercial Farms, Agribusinesses, Food Processors, Out-grower Networks, Agritech

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll get you more buyers"
A Lagos / SW commercial farm or food processor is not order-starved. They are OFF-TAKER coordination broken — wholesale buyers ghost on payment, restaurant buyers reorder via WhatsApp at chaotic hours, and cold-chain breaks because nobody acknowledged the delivery WhatsApp at 5am. Lead with the OFF-TAKER + back-office grind, not lead-gen.

### Who you're reaching
Commercial farms (poultry, fish, vegetable, fruit, livestock), food processors (juice / sauces / snacks / oils / poultry abattoirs / dairy), out-grower network coordinators, agritech platforms with field agents, agro-input dealers (seed / feed / agrochem), aquaculture (catfish / tilapia farms), greenhouse operations, organic farms supplying premium hospitality. Owners often spent 10+ years working another career; technically literate but back-office-overwhelmed. Often Ogun / Oyo / Lagos peri-urban with offices in Lagos.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A typical poultry farm: **₦2M–₦20M monthly revenue**, 5,000–50,000 birds in cycle. Off-takers (restaurants, supermarkets, retail wholesalers) order via WhatsApp; whoever responds + delivers fastest wins repeat business.
- Fish farm: **₦500K–₦8M monthly**. Catfish processors and restaurants order daily. Slow Sunday-evening response = next-day order to the competing pond.
- Food processor (e.g. tomato paste, sauces): **₦5M–₦50M monthly to FMCG distributors**. Orders, payment confirmations, dispatch coordination ALL on WhatsApp. Receivables pile up because nobody chases politely.
- Out-grower coordinator: 100–2,000 smallholder farmers reporting harvest, prices, quality issues. ALL on WhatsApp. Coordinator is the bottleneck.
- Agro-input dealer: **₦5K–₦200K per order**. Farmer asks "do you have NPK 20:10:10?", dealer replies tomorrow, farmer bought from another supplier already.
- Receivables — wholesale buyers in Mile 12 / Bodija / Onitsha buy on 14–30 day credit. Farms are bad at chasing. **30%+ of receivables go past 60 days** and need legal escalation.
- The kicker — farm owners are managing inputs, animal health, harvest timing, off-takers, and back-office payment chasing simultaneously. WhatsApp piles up. The "manager" they hire often has zero financial visibility, can't approve discounts.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"Off-takers welcome / wholesale enquiries via WhatsApp"** in IG bio — biggest tell.
- **Farm photos with capacity claims** ("5,000 birds weekly", "1,000kg fish output") — proves scale.
- **B2B buyer logos / mentions** ("supplying X supermarket chain", "official supplier to Y restaurant group") — high-volume B2B funnel.
- **NAFDAC / SON / FMARD certifications** displayed — premium tier, regulated.
- **Recent processing / cold-chain investment posts** — capacity to fill, every order matters.
- **AfCFTA export language** ("we export to Ghana / Senegal") — high-ticket, slow-payment B2B.
- **Out-grower network mentions** ("we work with 500 smallholder farmers") — coordination chaos.
- **Reviews complaining of late delivery / WhatsApp ignored** — public proof.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Subsistence-level farms <100 birds / no clear B2B buyers — too small.
- NGO-funded co-ops with no commercial off-take — wrong economics.
- Government schemes (CBN Anchor Borrowers' Programme intermediaries only) — wrong sales cycle.
- Pure brokers with no product — different model.
