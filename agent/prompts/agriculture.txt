## Vertical: Agriculture & Agribusiness — MarketOS

### Who you're reaching
Farmers, agribusiness operators, commodity traders, input suppliers, and cooperatives across Lagos and Southwest Nigeria. They buy and sell maize, cassava, tomatoes, palm oil, and other commodities — pricing based on gut feel and market gossip. Input costs (fertiliser, seeds, diesel) shift weekly and they often get caught buying high.

### Their pain
Price information is fragmented and slow. A trader in Mile 12 doesn't know what the same commodity is trading for in Shagamu or Ibadan today. Farmers buy inputs at the wrong time — missing price dips by days. Cooperative members don't know if their collective pricing is competitive. No early warning when a price spike is coming.

### What ReachNG sells them — MarketOS (₦10K–₦40K/month)

MarketOS is a price intelligence operating system for Nigerian commodity traders, farmers, and agribusinesses. It puts real-time market data directly into their WhatsApp.

**Commodity Price Intelligence** — Real-time price tracking for Lagos commodity markets (Mile 12, Oyingbo, Oshodi). Sends a daily WhatsApp briefing with price movements and trend direction. Traders stop relying on middlemen for pricing information.

**Farmer Input Cost Alert** — Monitors fertiliser, seed, and diesel prices. Sends a buy alert when prices dip below a threshold — before the trader's supplier adjusts. Prevents buying at the peak.

**Ankara / Fabric Price Alert** — For fabric and textile traders: monitors upstream fabric prices and notifies when to stock up or hold off. Same logic applied to the textile market.

**Market Price Aggregator** — Aggregates prices across multiple Lagos markets and presents a unified view. Farmers selling to middlemen can now negotiate from a position of knowledge.
