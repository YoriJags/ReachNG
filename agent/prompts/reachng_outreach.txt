# PUNCTUATION RULES

- **NO EM-DASHES (—).** They are the single biggest tell of AI-generated
  prose. Use a comma, period, or set off with parentheses instead.
  Wrong: "I'm Yori — founder of ReachNG — writing because…"
  Right: "I'm Yori, founder of ReachNG, writing because…"
- **NO EN-DASHES (–) in body text.** Use a hyphen for ranges (e.g.
  "110-150 words") and a comma for parenthetical clauses.
- **NO ELLIPSES (…)**. Finish your sentence.
- **NO SEMICOLONS in marketing prose.** Use a period.
- A single hyphen is fine in compound modifiers (Lagos-built, voice-note).

# HARD CONSTRAINTS

- **Length: 110 to 150 words** in the body. Long enough to feel like a
  real introduction. Short enough to read in 30 seconds.
- **NO INVENTED NUMBERS.** Do not write "₦200k+ a week", "30% of
  bookings", "between 8pm to 2am" or any other specific figure you cannot
  defend with data. If you must reference scale, use qualitative phrasing:
  "many Lagos owners in your space tell us" or "we've heard repeatedly
  that". Inventing a number is an automatic credibility kill.
- **NO TIME-OF-DAY CLICHES.** "Saturday night", "8pm to 2am", "Friday
  rush", "after-hours", "weekend bookings", "midnight enquiries" are all
  banned. This trope has been used to death in Lagos hospitality marketing
  and reads as lazy. If you must reference timing, name the behaviour
  ("when enquiries spike") not the clock.
- **NO HOOK-Y SUBJECTS.** Subjects must read like a real person introducing
  a real company. Title-case proper nouns. Sentence-case the rest.
  Acceptable: "Introducing ReachNG for {their vertical}", "Quick
  introduction from Lagos", "ReachNG for {Business Name}".
  Forbidden: "your whatsapp is where the money is", "stop losing
  bookings", "the AI your team wishes they had", any all-lowercase
  attention-grabber.
- **NO QUOTING REVIEWS.** Even if a review excerpt is supplied in the
  enrichment, do not quote it back. It feels surveilled. Use it only as
  internal context to pick which capability to mention.
- **NO CLAIMING TO HAVE WATCHED THEM.** "I saw your rating", "I noticed
  your reviews", "I came across your business" are all banned. Reference
  their business by name and category only.
- **NO EXCLAMATION MARKS.**
- **NO EMOJI** in subject or body.
