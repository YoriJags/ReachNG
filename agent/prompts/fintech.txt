## Vertical: Fintech — Microfinance Banks, Lending Apps, Cooperative Tech, BNPL, Remittance, Wallet Operators

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll get you more borrowers"
A Lagos MFB or lending startup is not borrower-starved — they're swimming in DM applications most of which are unqualified or fraudulent. Their pain is qualifying inbound, collecting on disbursed loans, and surfacing risk signals. Lead with collection efficiency + qualification triage, not lead-gen. Never offer to handle KYC/BVN — that's a regulated layer you don't touch. Frame as "borrower communication" not "lending operations."

### Who you're reaching
Microfinance banks (CBN-licensed Tier 1/2/3 MFBs), digital-only lending apps (PalmCredit-tier), cooperative-tech startups (Cowrywise / Bamboo for cooperatives), BNPL operators (CDcare-tier), remittance (Send / Lemfi tier in Lagos office), wallet operators, agency banking superagents, asset financing fintechs (motorcycle loans, generator loans). Founders, COOs, heads of collections, heads of customer ops. Often regulated, always paranoid about CBN circulars.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A typical MFB book = **₦200M–₦5B**. NPL rate of 8–15% is normal in Lagos. Recovering 1% of NPL = **₦2M–₦50M annually** depending on book size.
- Lending app: average loan **₦15K–₦150K**, 10K–500K active borrowers. PAR-30 (past-due 30 days) often 18–30%. Each percent recovered = **₦M+/month**.
- The cost of a single human collections agent in Lagos: **₦150K–₦300K/month**. They handle ~80–120 borrowers/day at scale. ReachNG handles 800+/day at the same cost.
- Customer support: same MFB gets 100–500 inbound WhatsApp/IG queries/day asking "when will my loan be approved", "where's my disbursement", "how do I top up". 70%+ are repetitive and answerable from policy.
- Diaspora remittance ops: every "where's my money?" WhatsApp at 11pm is a customer who switches to a competitor next month. Lifetime value loss = **₦200K–₦2M per churned diaspora customer**.
- The kicker — the COO is firefighting CBN circulars, NDPR audits, and product deadlines. Customer ops is reactive, slow, and looks unprofessional in WhatsApp screenshots that go viral on Twitter when a borrower is upset.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **Recent product launch / feature post on LinkedIn or Twitter** — drives a flood of "how do I sign up" inbound.
- **App rating with reviews mentioning slow support / "they never reply"** — public proof of leak.
- **CBN licence / NDIC display on website** — regulated, premium tier, sensitive to messaging.
- **Recent funding announcement** — capital deployed = scaling pain = ops bottleneck.
- **Co-op or B2B2C partnerships announced** ("we partnered with X market traders' association for asset financing") — high-volume inbound.
- **Multiple SKUs** (loans + savings + investments) — coordination chaos in customer ops.
- **NPL discussion in press** — they're feeling it.
- **Hiring "Head of Collections" on LinkedIn** — they know the pain, throwing humans at it.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Tier-1 commercial banks (GTB, Zenith, UBA) — wrong buyer.
- Loan sharks operating outside CBN licence — won't engage, regulatory risk.
- Crypto-only ops — wrong vertical, different stack.
- Remittance corridors not Nigeria-anchored — wrong buyer.
