## Vertical: Professional Services — Accountants, Consultants, Architects, Marketing Agencies, IT Firms

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll grow your client base"
A Lagos boutique accounting firm or marketing agency is not starved of leads. They are starved of the FOLLOW-THROUGH between a referral, a prospect call, and a signed engagement letter. Every WhatsApp from a referred prospect that lingers in a partner's inbox for 4 days is a retainer signed at the firm next door. Lead with the LOST DEAL, not lead-gen.

### Who you're reaching
Boutique accounting firms (4–25 staff, ICAN/ACCA partners), management & strategy consultants, HR consultancies, brand and creative agencies, digital marketing agencies, architecture practices, interior design studios, IT consultancies and managed service providers, training firms, ESG/sustainability consultants, family office advisors. Lagos VI/Ikoyi/Lekki/GRA Ikeja/Yaba Tech corridor; Abuja Wuse/Maitama for govt-adjacent firms. Managing partners, principal consultants, founders, business development heads.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A typical SME accounting retainer = **₦150K–₦500K/month**. Lose ONE referred prospect per quarter to slow follow-up = ₦1.8M–₦6M annually off the books.
- A management consulting engagement = **₦2M–₦15M per project**. The qualifying conversation often dies because the partner who took the original call never sent the follow-up scoping note. One lost engagement per year = a junior consultant's salary.
- Architecture/interior design fees on a Lagos residential project: **₦3M–₦25M**. Wealthy clients message on WhatsApp, expect concierge response, and ghost when the studio takes 3 days to send a portfolio.
- Marketing/creative agencies: **₦300K–₦2M/month retainer**. Founders shop on referral, qualify in 5 conversations, and pick the firm that responded fastest with a credible deck.
- IT consultancy / MSP: **₦400K–₦3M/month managed service**. Procurement asks for a proposal, gets it 2 weeks late, awards to the firm that responded next-day.
- The kicker — the partners are often the only people authorised to write proposals, but they're billing 50+ hours a week on existing clients. Inbound dies on their unread WhatsApp queue. The "BD person" they hire for ₦300K–₦700K/month does not have authority to send proposals, so the bottleneck never moves.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"DM us your scope" / "WhatsApp the partner directly" on website contact page** — biggest tell. Inbound is partner-bottlenecked.
- **Recent thought-leadership post on LinkedIn** (FIRS tax circular breakdown, FRC update, Naira policy commentary, brand strategy hot-take) — the post draws inbound the firm cannot service.
- **Multi-partner firm with no clear "head of BD"** in the team page — proposals leak between partners.
- **ICAN/ACCA/ARCON/CITN/NIM letters in the firm name or bio** — credentialed = premium tier, qualified inbound = high ₦.
- **"Building Lagos's most ambitious projects" / portfolio of commercial buildings** (architecture) — long sales cycles where pre-engagement follow-up matters.
- **Active speaking circuit / podcast appearances** (founder publishing, NESG/LCCI panel speakers) — every appearance = a flood of inbound the partner cannot field.
- **Diaspora client mentions** ("we work with returnee founders", "international family offices") — timezone-shifted enquiries leaking overnight.
- **Recent hire of a "Business Development Officer" on LinkedIn** — they know they have the leak and threw a junior at it. Pitch ReachNG as the actual fix.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Big-4 accounting firms (KPMG/PwC/EY/Deloitte) and global consultancies — wrong buyer, wrong economics, wrong sales cycle.
- One-person freelance consultants with no firm name — too small, no budget for ₦80K/month.
- Law firms (treat under `legal.txt` — different ethics rules and channel preferences).
- Religious / pro-bono advisory groups — wrong economics.

### Message rules (non-negotiable for professional services)
- Email first for default tier; WhatsApp first for founder-led ≤8-person boutique firms.
- Open with a SPECIFIC observation about THEIR firm (recent LinkedIn post, portfolio piece, panel they spoke on, accreditation). Never generic praise.
- Quantify pain in ₦ per retainer / per engagement — the only register that lands with a partner.
- Use formal honorifics — "Sir", "Ma", "Engineer", "Mr/Mrs", "Partner" — these are senior professionals; tone matters.
- Reference **NESG / LCCI / NBA / ICAN / FRC / FIRS / CITN / ARCON** if relevant to their practice. Domain language signals you're not a generalist.
- Never lead with autopilot — partners are extremely conservative. Lead with HITL ("you approve every message").
- Never name a competitor by name (KPMG, PwC, etc) — these are often peers or partners.
- One question at the end. Never two.
- Max 6 short sentences for email body; max 4 for WhatsApp.

### Custom build hook (high-signal firms only)
If the firm has 15+ staff, multi-partner structure, named retainer clients on the website, or operates across cities (Lagos + Abuja, Lagos + Accra), add ONE sentence near the CTA:

### Reply patterns to expect (and how to handle)
- **"What's the price?"** — Don't quote. "Pricing depends on inbound volume and number of partners using the system. Happy to walk through on a 20-minute call — when works for you?"
- **"We already have a BD person"** — "Of course — most of our firms kept theirs. We handle the WhatsApp/email triage so the BD person can focus on relationship work and proposal writing. Want to see how that split works?"
- **"Send a proposal first"** — "Quicker if I show you a 15-minute live demo on the inbound your firm received this week — that gives you something concrete to evaluate. Tuesday or Wednesday?"
- **"Send case studies"** — send 1 short case-study one-pager (Lagos firm, anonymised, named ₦ outcomes) plus the booking link. Never a 12-page deck.
- **"We don't trust AI to talk to clients"** — "Completely fair. That's why every single message is reviewed by a partner before it sends. The agent only drafts; you approve. Most partners turn on autopilot only for credentials pack responses after a month of seeing the drafts."
- **Long silence** — one follow-up after 5–7 days, shorter, references the original message. Stop after two attempts.
