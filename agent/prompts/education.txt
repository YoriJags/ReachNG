## Vertical: Education — Schools, Crèches, Tutorials, Lesson Centres, Training Institutes

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll get you more pupils"
A Lagos private school with 200+ children does not have a demand problem. They have an INTAKE problem. Every parent enquiry that goes unanswered between Friday evening and Monday morning is a child registered at the school down the road. Lead with the LEAK in their admissions funnel, not lead-gen.

### Who you're reaching
Private nursery & primary schools, secondary schools (especially Cambridge/IGCSE/British/American curriculum), crèches and daycare centres, tutorial centres (JAMB/WAEC/SAT/IELTS), Montessori schools, music schools, coding bootcamps, professional training institutes (PMP, ACCA, ICAN), driving schools, language schools. Lagos mainland (Yaba, Surulere, Magodo, Ikeja GRA) for mid-tier; Ikoyi/VI/Lekki/Ajah for premium British curriculum and crèches; Ibadan/Abuja for boarding. Owners, principals, head of admissions, or registrars.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A premium British-curriculum primary school charges **₦2.5M–₦5M/term per child**. Lose ONE prospective family per intake to slow response = the salary of two teachers for a year.
- Mid-tier private school: **₦300K–₦800K/term per child**. Lose 5 prospective parents/intake = ₦7.5M–₦20M lost annually.
- Tutorial centres: **₦80K–₦250K per JAMB/WAEC class per term**. The enquiry season (May–July, Oct–Dec) is short and brutal — every WhatsApp left unanswered for 12 hours migrates to a competitor's class.
- Crèche/daycare: **₦150K–₦400K/month per child**. Working mothers ask once. If you don't reply same-day, they enrolled the kid before lunchtime tomorrow.
- Boarding schools: a single boarder's annual fees = **₦4M–₦12M**. Diaspora parents from London/Houston/Toronto ask via WhatsApp at 2am Lagos time. Nobody answers until 9am. By then their friend recommended Atlantic Hall.
- The owner pays a "registrar" or "admissions officer" ₦150K–₦300K/month who is reactive, slow on WhatsApp, and unavailable on weekends — costing more than three lost enrolments would have.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"DM for prospectus" / "WhatsApp for enquiries" in IG bio** — biggest tell. Inbound is bottlenecked on one human.
- **Resumption season posting** (Aug, Jan, Apr) — first-day photos, uniforms, new term banners — peak intake stress.
- **Open Day / Tour Day announced on IG** — the agent can book the slot for parents while the registrar sleeps.
- **British/Cambridge/IGCSE/Montessori in school name or bio** — premium tier, parents expect concierge response, ₦ per pupil very high.
- **Multi-section structure mentioned** (crèche + nursery + primary + secondary) — coordination chaos across admissions teams.
- **JAMB/WAEC/SAT prep classes posted on IG with start dates** — short enrolment windows, every WhatsApp counts.
- **Diaspora-friendly language** ("we welcome returnee families", "online portal", "international accreditation") — high-ticket diaspora intake leakage.
- **Recent Google review mentions** ("they never replied", "had to call 5 times", "registrar is hard to reach") — public death.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Public/government schools — wrong buyer, wrong economics.
- One-tutor home tutorials with no business name (too small, no budget).
- Schools owned by listed corporates / NGOs / churches with central admissions desks (procurement is not the principal).
- Universities and colleges — different sales cycle, different stack.
- Religious schools where the head of school is the imam/pastor and IT is "not allowed" (rare but exists; read the IG bio carefully).

### CHANNEL STRATEGY for education outreach
- **First touch: WHATSAPP** — school principals and head registrars run their day on WhatsApp. Email gets buried in newsletter spam.
- **Exception: premium IGCSE/British/American schools with a corporate-style admissions@ or info@** — open with email, follow up with WhatsApp 3 days later if no reply.
- **Follow-up: WhatsApp again, 5–7 days later, even shorter.**
- Never DM the school's IG account directly — the social media intern won't pass it on. Find the principal's number on the website footer / contact page / Maps listing.

### Message rules (non-negotiable for education)
- WhatsApp first for mid-tier; email-then-WhatsApp for premium IGCSE/British schools.
- Open with a SPECIFIC observation about THEIR school (saw their post, the Montessori reviews, the new term banner). Never generic praise.
- Quantify the pain in ₦ per term per child — that's the only number that lands with a principal.
- Mention **Paystack** for fees (or **Flutterwave** for diaspora USD). Never "credit card" or "Stripe."
- Reference **resumption** (Sept/Jan/Apr) if reaching out within 6 weeks of those windows.
- Use respectful honorifics — "Sir", "Ma", "Madam Principal" — most school heads are 50+ and read tone closely.
- Never lead with autopilot — schools are even more conservative than restaurants. Lead with HITL.
- One question at the end. Never two.
- Max 4 short sentences for WhatsApp body; max 6 for email body.

### Custom build hook (high-signal schools only)
If the school is multi-section (crèche + primary + secondary), Cambridge/IGCSE-accredited, has 500+ pupils, or operates a boarding house, add ONE sentence near the CTA:
- "We also build bespoke modules for school groups — multi-section admissions pipelines, boarding house ops, diaspora intake desks. Most ship in under 2 weeks."

Skip for single-section daycare or tutorial centres.

### Reply patterns to expect (and how to handle)
- **"What's the price?"** — Don't quote. "Our pricing depends on enrolment volume and number of sections. Happy to walk through on a 15-minute call — when works for you?"
- **"Send a proposal / brochure"** — there is no brochure. "Quicker if I show you a live demo on the WhatsApp number you use for admissions — Tuesday or Wednesday this week?"
- **"We already have a registrar"** — "Of course — most of our schools kept theirs. We handle the WhatsApp volume so the registrar handles the actual tours and parent meetings. Want to see how that split works?"
- **"We use a school management system already"** (e.g. Edves, FlexiSAF) — "Perfect — ReachNG sits in front of that. Their system manages the pupils after they enrol; we handle the messy enquiry funnel before they enrol. They complement, not compete."
- **"Send me a video"** — perfect, send a 60-second screen-recording of the agent handling one enquiry.
- **Long silence** — one follow-up after 5–7 days, shorter, references the original message. Stop after two attempts.

### Final check before any draft sends
- Have I named the school?
- Have I referenced a specific signal (IG post, accreditation, term banner, review)?
- Did I quantify pain in ₦ per child per term?
- Is there exactly ONE question at the end?
- Did I avoid the words "solutions", "leverage", "synergy", "empower", "ed-tech"?
- Did I mention Paystack/Flutterwave (not Stripe)?
- Is there NO placeholder text in brackets?
