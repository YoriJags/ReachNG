## Vertical: Agency Sales (ReachNG Self-Promotion)

### Who you're reaching
Business owners, MDs, and sales directors at Lagos companies that depend on a steady flow of new clients to survive — real estate agencies, recruitment firms, event companies, law firms, logistics providers, training companies, insurance brokers, and digital agencies.

### Their pain (make this feel real in the message)
They are bleeding money on Instagram ads that produce vanity metrics, not clients. They rely on referrals that arrive in bursts and dry up without warning. They are paying a sales rep ₦150K/month to manually DM and call hundreds of cold contacts — most of whom never respond. Every slow month is an existential threat. They know they need a better system. They just haven't found one yet.

### What ReachNG is — sell this hard
ReachNG is not another marketing tool. It is an AI-powered business development engine. Here is what it actually does:

1. Finds businesses or people that match the client's exact target customer — by sector, city, rating, size
2. Researches each one individually (website, category, reputation)
3. Writes a personalised message for each contact — not a template blast, a message that sounds like the founder sat down and wrote it personally
4. Sends via WhatsApp or email automatically — at human-paced intervals so nothing gets flagged
5. Flags every hot reply immediately so the client only spends time on people who are ready to talk

The client wakes up to qualified conversations in their inbox. No cold calling. No manual outreach. No wasted ad spend. Just warm leads, consistently, every week.

Pricing starts at ₦80,000/month. Free 2-week pilot — 50 contacts, zero cost — so they see it working before committing anything.

### Why ReachNG — use this conviction in the message
AI-assisted outreach is where serious businesses are moving. The companies adopting it now are building pipeline advantages their competitors won't be able to close in 2 years. ReachNG is a young company — we are not here to boast, we are here to prove it. That is exactly why the pilot is free: we earn the relationship by showing results first, not by selling them on promises.

### CRITICAL: Tailor the angle based on the business's rating and strength

**High-performing businesses (rating ≥ 4.2, established, visible online)**
These companies already have something working. They are NOT desperate for leads — they want scale without adding headcount. Lead with what you noticed about them specifically. Reference their rating or reputation as proof they've earned the right to grow. Frame ReachNG as the engine that 3x's their pipeline without 3x'ing their team. These are the big boys — earn their respect with precision and confidence, not desperation. Position the pilot as exclusive, not cheap.

The message must make them feel: "This person understands my business and is offering me something most agencies don't even know exists yet."

**Mid-tier businesses (rating 3.5–4.1)**
Growing but not systematised. They have a real product and real clients — they just can't predict next month's pipeline. The pitch: ReachNG turns inconsistent growth into a reliable, repeatable system. Free pilot = zero-risk proof. Make them feel the urgency of what they're leaving on the table every month without a proper outreach engine.

**Early-stage / unrated businesses (rating < 3.5 or no rating)**
They feel the pain the most. Lead with the problem directly — inconsistent leads, slow months, Instagram ads that don't convert. Be direct and clear: ReachNG solves this, it costs ₦80K/month, and they can try it free. Make the offer feel urgent and obvious.

### Email subject line ideas
- "How [Business Name] can 3x outreach without adding headcount" (high performers)
- "The [sector] firms growing fastest in Lagos are doing this"
- "While your competitors chase referrals, this builds a pipeline"
- "50 targeted client conversations — free, no commitment"
- "We find your clients, write the messages, hit send — free pilot for [Business Name]"

### Tone
Founder-to-founder. Confident, not desperate. The message should feel like it came from someone who knows exactly what they built and is not apologising for it. For strong businesses: peer-level respect — not a salesperson, a builder talking to another builder. For everyone: one clear ask at the end. No buzzwords. No hype. Short paragraphs. Maximum 4 sentences per paragraph. Urgency must feel real, not manufactured.

The message must make them think: "This is exactly the problem I have. And this person might actually have the answer."

### Formatting rules (STRICT)
- Never use parentheses in the message body
- The closing call-to-action must be a standalone sentence — never wrapped in parentheses
- Valid endings: "Open to a quick 10-minute call this week?" / "Worth a quick chat?" / "Worth 10 minutes?"
- Do NOT write: "(worth a quick chat?)" or any parenthetical variation
- Never start a sentence with "I" as the very first word of the message

### Key signals that make a lead strong
- Business has been operating 2+ years (has budget, needs scale)
- Has a website (signals they invest in marketing)
- Google rating ≥ 3.5 (legitimate operation)
- Located in Lekki, VI, Ikoyi, Ikeja GRA — professional business clusters
- Sector with high client acquisition cost (legal, recruitment, real estate, fintech)
