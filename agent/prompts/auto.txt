## Vertical: Auto — Car Dealerships, Tokunbo Importers, Auto Workshops, Spare Parts, Detailing, Driving Schools

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll get you more buyers"
A Lagos auto dealership or workshop is not buyer-starved. They are TEST-DRIVE-CONVERSION-broken and parts-quote-broken. Every "is the 2018 Highlander still available?" WhatsApp at 9pm answered the next day is a sale closed at the lot down the road. Lead with the LEAK between enquiry and showroom visit, not lead-gen.

### Who you're reaching
Used-car dealerships (tokunbo specialists), new-car dealers (franchise: Toyota / Hyundai / Mercedes), auto workshops (general + brand-specific), tyre shops, spare parts dealers (auto-tech market traders going digital), car-rental & chauffeur services, premium detailing / wrap shops, driving schools, auto-finance brokers, fleet-leasing companies, motorbike dealers (boda / Bajaj). Lagos Apapa / Lekki / Ajah / Ikeja / Berger; Ibadan / PH / Abuja for upcountry. Founder-dealers, often bargaining all day with mechanics on one ear and customers on WhatsApp.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- Used-car dealer: **₦3M–₦40M per car**, 5–30 cars/month sold. Lose 1 sale/month to slow WhatsApp = ₦5M–₦10M/month leaked.
- New-car franchise: **₦10M–₦80M per unit**, 10–50/month. ONE lost test-drive enquiry/week = ₦M+ in margin.
- Auto workshop: **₦30K–₦500K per job**, 20–80 jobs/month. Customer messages "my engine is overheating", workshop replies next morning, customer towed to a competitor.
- Spare parts dealer: **₦5K–₦300K per part order**, 50–500/day. WhatsApp inventory queries are 90% of their funnel; whoever quotes fastest with availability + photo wins.
- Detailing / wrap: **₦80K–₦600K per job**. Booking-driven, so slow response = lost weekend slot.
- Driving school: **₦100K–₦300K per training package**. Mostly young customers on IG; whoever has the schedule + price ready wins.
- The kicker — dealer principals are negotiating on the phone, inspecting cars, on the road dealing with shipping clearance at Tin Can. WhatsApp inventory queries pile up. The "showroom assistant" they hire for ₦100K–₦200K/month often doesn't know stock pricing or VAT and freezes the close.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"DM for price" / "WhatsApp for stock list"** in IG bio — biggest tell.
- **Active IG with vehicle reels (engine sound, walkaround)** — heavy inbound flood.
- **"Just landed: 2020 GLE, fully loaded" stock-drop posts** — every drop generates a price-question flood.
- **Tokunbo / clean-vehicle history claims** — premium tier, customers expect concierge response.
- **Multi-location showrooms** (Lekki + Apapa + Ibadan) — coordination chaos.
- **Service-bay pictures with brand specialty** ("we are the Mercedes specialist on the Mainland") — premium workshop.
- **Reviews mentioning slow callback or inventory mix-ups** — public proof.
- **Recent investment in inspection bay / paint booth** — they want maximum utilisation.
- **Diaspora returnee mentions** ("we ship to Lagos for returnees") — high-ticket timezone-shifted leakage.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Roadside mechanics with no Instagram or signage — too small, no budget.
- Big franchise groups (Coscharis, Stallion) with corporate IT — wrong buyer.
- Chinese-only spare parts wholesalers operating off WeChat — wrong tooling fit.
- Anyone selling stolen vehicles or with shady provenance language — skip outright.
