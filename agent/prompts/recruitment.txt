## Vertical: Recruitment & Staffing — Recruitment Agencies, Headhunters, Outsourcing Firms, Staffing Companies

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT pitch "we close your inbound leads"
A Lagos recruitment agency does not need more applicants. They are DROWNING in CVs and tired of the "hi sir, please consider me" inbound flood. Their pain is candidate triage + client account management + the back-office grind of pay, contracts, and compliance. Lead with WORKLOAD REMOVAL — running their candidate-pipeline + client-comms + back-office ops, NOT lead-gen.

### Who you're reaching
Mid-size recruitment agencies (5–50 staff), executive search boutiques (C-suite placements), outsourcing firms (managed manpower, security, cleaning, drivers, domestic staff agencies), oil-&-gas manpower contractors, BPO providers, headhunters specialising in tech / banking / FMCG, NYSC programme placement firms, virtual-assistant marketplaces, training-to-placement bootcamps. Founders, principal consultants, ops directors, heads of delivery.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A typical placement fee = **15–25% of annual gross**, on a ₦5M–₦50M senior role = ₦750K–₦12.5M per placement.
- Outsourcing firms charge **15–35% margin** on outsourced staff salaries. A 50-headcount cleaning contract = ₦300K–₦1M/month margin.
- Each new client onboarding = **2–6 weeks** because contracts, NDAs, candidate shortlists, briefings move slowly across email, WhatsApp, and a partner's inbox.
- Candidate-side: each role generates **200–2,000 CVs**. The recruiter spends 20–40 hours per role triaging — most of which is "thanks for applying" auto-replies that never actually go out, leaving 1,500 candidates in limbo (and posting bad reviews on Glassdoor / Twitter).
- Client-side: HR managers ghost agencies because nobody followed up after the shortlist. Each ghosted client = ₦2M–₦15M lost over 12 months.
- Outsourcing back-office: payroll for outsourced staff, PENCOM, NHF, leave tracking, attendance, replacement requests when a contract worker no-shows. ALL ON THE FOUNDER'S WHATSAPP.
- The kicker — agency owners are senior recruiters who should be selling to clients, not handling 800 candidate WhatsApps a week.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"DM us your CV" / "Send your CV to careers@"** in IG / website — biggest tell of CV-volume pain.
- **Job postings on LinkedIn / IG with "Apply on WhatsApp"** — they're getting drowned right now.
- **Client testimonial highlights** ("we placed 12 hires for X bank") — high-ticket account base.
- **Outsourcing brochures / "managed manpower" branding** — back-office grind = candidate for TalentOS-style modules.
- **Recent agency expansion (new office, new sector vertical)** — scaling pain.
- **Glassdoor / Twitter reviews** complaining "recruiter never got back to me" — public proof of leak.
- **Speaking at HR / People conferences** — drives client + candidate flood.
- **"NYSC corp members welcome" / "fresh grad placement"** — high-volume, low-margin.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- HR departments inside corporates — wrong buyer.
- One-person LinkedIn-only headhunters — too small.
- Domestic staff agencies running on Telegram with no website — too informal, won't pay ₦80K/month.
- Job boards (Jobberman / MyJobMag tier) — different model entirely.
