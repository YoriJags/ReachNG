## Vertical: Legal — Boutique Law Firms, Solo Practitioners, Chambers, Legal Consultancies

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll bring you more clients"
A reputable Lagos boutique law firm does not lack matters. They lack a system that captures the inbound enquiry, qualifies the matter, sends the engagement letter, and collects the retainer BEFORE the prospect quietly briefs another counsel. Lead with the unsigned engagement, not lead-gen. NBA Rules of Professional Conduct prohibit certain forms of solicitation — be careful. Frame as "back-office automation," not "client acquisition."

### Who you're reaching
Boutique commercial law firms (3–25 lawyers), litigation practices, corporate/M&A boutiques, family law / probate practices, immigration law firms, real estate / conveyancing chambers, IP boutiques, employment law specialists, dispute resolution & arbitration practices, in-house compliance consultants spinning out. Lagos VI / Ikoyi / Ikeja GRA / Lekki Phase 1 for premium tier; Marina / Yaba / Surulere for mid-tier; Abuja Wuse 2 / Maitama for govt-adjacent firms. Managing partners, principal partners, founding partners, sometimes a senior associate running BD.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A typical Lagos commercial retainer = **₦300K–₦1.5M/month**. Lose ONE referred matter per quarter to slow follow-up = ₦3.6M–₦18M annually off the books.
- A litigation matter brief = **₦1M–₦15M+** depending on complexity. The qualifying conversation often dies because the partner who took the original call never sent the engagement letter.
- Real estate / conveyancing files: **₦200K–₦2M per transaction**. Estate agents and developers send WhatsApp briefs daily; whoever responds within an hour gets the file.
- Family law / probate: **₦500K–₦5M per matter**. Diaspora clients ask via WhatsApp at 2am Lagos. Nobody answers until 9am. They retained UK-based counsel by lunchtime.
- Immigration: **₦300K–₦3M per case**. Time-sensitive (visa deadlines), and the firm that responds same-day signs the client.
- The kicker — partners are often the ONLY ones authorised to sign engagement letters and quote fees, but they're billable on existing matters. Inbound dies on their unread WhatsApp queue. The "BD officer" or junior associate they hire for ₦200K–₦500K/month cannot quote fees, so the bottleneck never moves.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **Recent thought-leadership on LinkedIn** (FIRS circular note, NDPR commentary, FRC update, CAMA analysis) — drives partner-bottlenecked inbound.
- **Active speaking circuit** (NBA section panels, NESG, LCCI, AfCFTA conferences) — every appearance generates inbound the partner cannot field.
- **Multi-partner chambers** with no clear "head of BD" on the team page — proposals leak between partners.
- **Practice area declared on website** (corporate, IP, immigration, probate) — lets you frame the pain specifically.
- **"For enquiries, contact info@" or "WhatsApp the partner directly"** on contact page — biggest tell of partner bottleneck.
- **Diaspora client mentions** ("we work with returnee families", "international corporate clients", visa/immigration practice) — timezone-shifted inbound leakage.
- **Recent associate hire on LinkedIn** — chambers expanding capacity, BD bottleneck still unresolved.
- **Firm name carries SAN, FCIArb, FCTI, ACIArb post-nominals** — premium tier, qualified inbound, high ₦ per matter.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Big-firm letterheads (Banwo & Ighodalo, Aluko & Oyebode, Templars, Olaniwun Ajayi tier) — wrong buyer, internal IT, procurement.
- Solo lawyers with no firm name and no website — too small.
- Federal / state ministry legal departments — wrong buyer.
- Pro-bono / NGO legal clinics — wrong economics.
- Chambers tied to political figures or that handle only government briefs — different sales cycle entirely.

### Message rules (non-negotiable for legal)
- Email first; WhatsApp only as follow-up or for founder-led ≤5-lawyer firms.
- **Never frame as "client acquisition" — NBA rules.** Frame as "inbound triage" or "back-office automation."
- **Never have the agent quote fees.** Always: "Partner will set fees at consultation."
- Open with a SPECIFIC observation about THEIR firm (recent LinkedIn post, panel, NBA section role, accreditation). Never generic praise.
- Quantify pain in ₦ per matter / per retainer — the only register that lands.
- Use formal honorifics — "Sir", "Ma", "Esq.", "SAN" where applicable.
- Reference **NBA, FRC, SEC, NDPR, CAMA, FIRS, AfCFTA, FCIArb** if relevant. Domain language signals you're not a generalist.
- Never lead with autopilot — partners are extremely conservative. Lead with HITL.
- Never name a peer firm — these are colleagues.
- One question at the end. Never two.
- Max 6 short sentences for email body; max 4 for WhatsApp.

### Custom build hook (high-signal firms only)
If the firm has 10+ lawyers, multi-practice structure, named retainer corporate clients on the website, or operates across cities (Lagos + Abuja), add ONE sentence near the CTA:
- "We also build bespoke modules for multi-partner chambers — practice-area routing, partner-shared client desks, retainer billing reminders, NDPR audit pulses. Most ship in under 2 weeks."

Skip for sole practitioners.

### Reply patterns to expect (and how to handle)
- **"What's the price?"** — "Pricing depends on inbound volume and number of partners using the system. Happy to walk through on a 20-minute call — when works for you?"
- **"Is this NBA-compliant?"** — "Yes — the agent triages inbound that already came to your firm. It does not solicit. It does not quote fees. It does not advise. It captures the brief and books the consultation, all under your supervision. Every message is reviewed by a partner before it sends."
- **"How does this comply with client confidentiality?"** — "The agent never stores matter substance, only intake metadata (name, contact, matter type, urgency, preferred slot). Substantive conversation happens at the consultation."
- **"We already have a BD officer / junior associate"** — "Of course — most of our firms kept theirs. The associate handles relationship work; the agent handles the WhatsApp/email triage volume. Want to see how that split works?"
- **"Send a brief"** — "Quicker if I show you a 15-minute live demo on the WhatsApp number you use for chambers — Tuesday or Wednesday this week?"
- **Long silence** — one follow-up after 7 days, shorter, references the original message. Stop after two attempts. Legal partners take the long view.
