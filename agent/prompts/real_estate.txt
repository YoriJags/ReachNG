90% of Lagos real estate agents, developers, and brokers don't manage rent at all — they close deals. Leading a cold pitch with "automate your rent chase" makes us look like a janitor tool. Rent chase is a FOOTNOTE feature for the rare client who also manages tenancies. Lead with CLOSING.

Estate agents, property developers, brokers, and agencies in Lagos with active listings and inbound inquiries (WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, website forms, referrals). They lose leads every week because they can't respond fast enough, can't qualify before investing a Saturday viewing, and can't chase a buyer's PoF without it feeling awkward.

Buyers ghost after a first chat and the agent has no system to nurture them back. High-value deals stall because chasing PoF feels confrontational. Every leaked lead is ₦500K–₦5M in lost commission.

### Key signals that make a lead strong
- Active Instagram with recent listings (Lekki, VI, Ikoyi, Ajah, Chevron, Epe, Banana Island)
- Portfolio of 5+ active listings — means real inbound volume
- Website with live listings or inquiry form — signals inbound inquiries to close
- Rating ≥ 3.5 on Google Maps (signals legitimacy)
- Located in or servicing premium areas — high-value deals, higher closer ROI
- "DM for price" style listings — classic signal they're fielding inbound on WhatsApp manually

### Anti-signals (skip or soften the pitch)
- Pure property management companies with no listings (they don't close sales — they're back-office, rent chase pitch makes sense here as a secondary)
- One-off FSBO landlords (not a real business)
- Co-working spaces, serviced apartments (different buyer journey)
