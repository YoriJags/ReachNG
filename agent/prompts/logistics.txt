## Vertical: Logistics — Last-mile Dispatch, Haulage, Inter-state Trucking, Cold-chain, 3PL

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll get you more shipments"
Lagos logistics SMEs are not shipment-starved. They are quote-conversion-broken. Every B2B WhatsApp asking for a quote at 9pm that's answered at 10am the next day is a job awarded to the dispatch firm with a faster reply. Lead with the LOST QUOTE + the chaos in their dispatch coordination — not lead-gen.

### Who you're reaching
Last-mile dispatch firms (10–200 riders), inter-state haulage operators (5–80 trucks), cold-chain (vaccines / pharma / frozen food), 3PL warehouses & fulfilment centres serving D2C brands, e-commerce dispatch partners (Jumia/Konga sellers' own riders), specialised cargo (oil & gas, project cargo), bonded warehouses, customs & freight forwarders, intra-Lagos courier services (KwikQuick / Gokada-tier competitors), bus/charter operators. Founders or operations heads, often ex-corporate haulage planners.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- Last-mile dispatch firm: revenue per delivery **₦1.5K–₦5K**, 100–500 deliveries/day. Lose 10 corporate accounts to slow quote response = ₦300K+/day in recurring revenue migrating elsewhere.
- Inter-state haulage: per trip Lagos–Onitsha = **₦400K–₦900K**, Lagos–Kano = **₦800K–₦2.5M**. ONE lost weekly job = ₦2M+ monthly leak.
- Cold-chain pharma run: **₦150K–₦600K per trip**. High-value, low-volume, hyper-time-sensitive. Whoever quotes within the hour wins the contract.
- 3PL fulfilment for D2C brands: **₦40–₦200 per pick-pack**, monthly retainer **₦150K–₦2M per brand**. Lose ONE brand/quarter to slow B2B onboarding response = ₦2M–₦24M annual leak.
- Customs/freight: **₦200K–₦5M per consignment cleared**. Procurement asks for quote, gets it 4 days later, awards to next-fastest forwarder.
- The kicker — operations head is on the radio with drivers + on the phone with customers + on WhatsApp quoting jobs simultaneously. Quotes get half-typed and never sent. Customers ghost. Trips dispatch late. Driver / vehicle utilisation drops.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"WhatsApp for quote" / "DM for rates"** in IG bio or website footer — biggest tell.
- **Recent fleet-expansion post** (new truck delivery, new bike batch, new cold van) — capacity to fill, every quote counts.
- **"Now servicing Abuja / PH / Ibadan corridor" announcements** — new lane = new B2B inbound = bottleneck stress.
- **Jumia / Konga / Glovo / Bumpa / e-commerce partnerships** mentioned — high-volume B2B inbound.
- **"Cold-chain certified" / NAFDAC compliance language** — premium tier, contracted accounts, pricing on confidence.
- **Case-study posts about big shipments** ("delivered 500 cartons to Aba in 18 hours") — drives B2B enquiries.
- **Multiple specialty mentions** (haulage + 3PL + last-mile) — coordination chaos.
- **Corporate logos in highlights** ("our partners: PZ, Nestlé, Unilever") — high-ticket B2B funnel.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Big haulage groups (DHL, GIG, Red Star) — wrong buyer, internal IT.
- Owner-driver of a single truck — too small.
- Customs agents who only do passenger luggage — different model.
