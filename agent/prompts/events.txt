## Vertical: Events — Event Planners, Wedding Planners, Decorators, Caterers, MCs, Event Venues, Owambe Specialists

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll get you more weddings"
A reputable Lagos events vendor is not booking-starved during owambe season — they are DEPOSIT-CHASING-broken and timeline-coordination-broken. Every "send me your packages" WhatsApp at 10pm that's answered the next day is a wedding date locked at the planner next door. Lead with the QUOTE-TO-DEPOSIT gap and the messy multi-vendor coordination — not lead-gen.

### Who you're reaching
Wedding planners (the principal coordinator), decorators, MCs, event caterers (often differentiated from restaurant caterers), photo & video houses, makeup artists for events, owambe-specialist DJs, party-rental firms (chairs / canopies / cutlery), event venues (banquet halls, marquees, beachfronts), bridal boutiques, aso-ebi coordinators, private chefs for events, corporate-event-management firms. Lagos VI / Ikoyi / Lekki / Ikeja for premium; Surulere / Yaba / Ajah for mid-tier; Abuja Wuse / Maitama / Asokoro for diplomatic + corporate events. Founder-coordinators who are also the senior planner.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- Lagos premium wedding planning fee = **₦2M–₦15M+** per wedding. Lose ONE date per year to slow follow-up = a junior planner's annual salary.
- Decoration & rentals: **₦800K–₦8M per wedding**. Owambe season (Aug–Dec): vendor calendars fill 6 months out. Every leaked enquiry = a Saturday lost.
- Catering: **₦8K–₦25K per head** × 200–800 guests = **₦1.6M–₦20M per event**. 50% deposit at booking. Slow quote = lost contract.
- Photo/video premium: **₦600K–₦4M per wedding**. Couples shop 6–8 photographers, pick the one who replied fastest with portfolio + price.
- Event venues: weekend booking **₦500K–₦5M**. Sunday slots fill 4–6 months ahead. Slow venue inspection scheduling = direct ₦ loss.
- The kicker — owambe season inbound is 5–10× normal. The principal planner is at a wedding every Saturday. WhatsApp goes unanswered Friday-Sunday because they're literally on the venue floor. Bookings for next year are decided this weekend.
- Multi-vendor chaos — wedding planners juggle 8–15 vendors per event in 8–15 different WhatsApp threads. Decorator forgot the chairs, caterer needs final headcount Tuesday, MC wants the running order, photographer wants the shot list. The planner's WhatsApp becomes ungovernable.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"DM for booking" / "WhatsApp the planner" in IG bio** — biggest tell.
- **Wedding portfolio reels with venue tags + couple tags** — heavy inbound flood.
- **"Owambe ready" / "December weddings open"** — peak season prep.
- **Aso-ebi coordination posts** — high-touch coordination job.
- **Multi-service offering** (planning + decor + catering or planning + MC + photo) — coordination chaos = clear ReachNG fit.
- **Diaspora wedding mentions** ("we plan weddings for returnee couples", "destination Lagos") — timezone-shifted intake leak.
- **High-NW venue tags** (Eko Hotel, Civic Centre, Le Meridien Ibom, private estates) — premium tier, big tickets.
- **Reviews complaining of slow follow-up or "took ages to confirm"** — public proof of leak.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- One-person side-hustle planners doing 3 events/year — too small.
- Pure venue groups (large hotels with internal events team) — wrong buyer.
- Religious-only event planners (church-tied, narrow market) — fine but smaller ₦.
- Big-name celeb planners with 50+ staff — wrong economics, internal IT.
