## Vertical: Cooperatives & Thrift — Esusu, Ajo, Multipurpose Co-ops, Trade Associations, Estate Co-ops, Workplace Thrift Schemes

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll get you more members"
A Lagos thrift / esusu / co-op secretary is not member-starved. They are CONTRIBUTION-CHASING-broken and member-comms-broken. Every "remember me, I'm waiting for my hand" complaint at 6am eats the secretary's whole day. Lead with the contribution-chase grind and the "where is my hand?" panic, not lead-gen.

### Who you're reaching
Multipurpose cooperative societies (registered under Cooperative Societies Act), workplace thrift schemes (corporate / civil-service / NYSC), market-trader association co-ops (Mile 12, Balogun, Computer Village, Aba sub-associations), estate / residential cooperative societies (gated estate ajo for service charges, generator levies), professional cooperatives (NMA, NBA branches, faculty co-ops), digital esusu/ajo platforms (PiggyVest cooperative competitors), informal "Sunday club" thrift groups operating digitally now. Secretaries, treasurers, presidents — usually unpaid volunteers running a 200–2000 member group on WhatsApp.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A typical mid-size co-op: **₦5K–₦200K monthly contribution per member × 200–2,000 members = ₦1M–₦400M/month under management**.
- Contribution chase: **20–40% of members miss the contribution date**. Secretary spends 3–5 evenings/week chasing on WhatsApp, often with the same scolding message copy-pasted 80 times.
- "Where is my hand?" — when a member's turn comes for the rotation payout, they message every day until they receive it. Secretary can spend half an hour answering "Aunty I haven't received it yet" 12 times.
- Reconciliation chaos — payments come via bank transfer, OPay, Paystack, sometimes cash to the treasurer. Matching to the right member every month takes the secretary 6–10 hours.
- Member onboarding: form-filling, KYC photo, NoK details. Done over WhatsApp messages → spreadsheet → registry. Each onboarding takes 30–45 minutes of secretary time.
- Loan request comms: members ask "can I get a soft loan", secretary explains the process 50 times a year.
- The kicker — secretaries are usually unpaid or paid <₦50K/month honorarium. They burn out in 18 months. Co-op trust collapses when they leave because the WhatsApp + spreadsheet system was all in their head.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **WhatsApp group link** in IG bio / website — biggest tell of WhatsApp-coordination pain.
- **"Contribution dates: 1st–5th every month"** posted publicly — proof of fixed cadence.
- **Member testimonial posts** ("collected my hand, thanks to the team") — proves rotation activity.
- **Multipurpose offerings** (savings + loans + soft loans + welfare) — coordination chaos.
- **Co-op registration number visible** — formal society, regulated.
- **Trade-association tied co-ops** (market trader unions) — high-volume, high-discipline.
- **Estate co-op tied to a gated community** — premium, predictable contribution schedule.
- **Active social-media announcements** about meetings, dues, AGM — communication intensity.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Pure informal "Sunday club" with no name or registration — too small.
- Anything claiming high guaranteed returns (Ponzi-adjacent) — skip outright.
- Tier-1 microfinance institutions presenting as co-ops — wrong category, use fintech.txt.
