## Vertical: Small Business — IG/TikTok-led SMEs, Beauty, Salons, Boutiques, D2C Brands, Home Services

### HARD TRUTH — Do NOT lead with "we'll grow your sales"
A Lagos D2C founder doing ₦5M–₦40M/month in IG-driven sales is not lead-starved. Their DM box has 200+ unread messages. Every "how much?" left unanswered for 3 hours is a customer who screenshotted, walked next door, or DM'd a competitor. Lead with the LEAK in their DM funnel, not lead-gen.

### Who you're reaching
Beauty brands (skincare, cosmetics, haircare), wig shops, fashion brands & boutiques, jewellery makers, home-goods D2C, food brands (small-batch sauces, baked goods, snacks), salons & spas, nail bars, lash & brow studios, barbers, makeup artists, photographers, party-planners, cake makers, home services (cleaning, pest control, AC repair, plumbing), gym studios under 100 members, mini-marts, online bookstores. Lagos Lekki / VI / Ikeja / Surulere / Yaba; founders 22–40, IG-native, mostly female-founder-led for beauty. Owner-operator typically takes every DM personally.

### Their real pain (quantified in ₦, not abstract)
- A typical beauty brand: average order = **₦12K–₦40K**, 30–80 DMs/day. Lose 5 orders/day to slow response = **₦60K–₦200K/day = ₦1.8M–₦6M/month** leaked.
- Wig shop: average ticket **₦80K–₦450K**. ONE lost wig sale per day = ₦80K, that's ₦2.4M/month. Wig customers are notoriously price-sensitive and shop 4–5 vendors in a day — first to reply with photo + price wins.
- Fashion brand: order **₦15K–₦150K**. They lose orders during owambe season because the seamstress can't reply DMs while sewing. ₦3M–₦8M/month leaked over weddings season.
- Salon / spa: appointment **₦8K–₦80K**. No-show rate of 25–35% because no deposit taken at booking. Each empty chair = ₦15K opportunity cost.
- Home services (cleaning, AC repair): job **₦20K–₦200K**. Customer asks via WhatsApp at 8pm, no reply till morning, books a competitor by 9am.
- The kicker — most founders themselves are the ONLY person answering DMs. They burn out, sleep with phone in hand, miss the gym, miss family time. They've considered hiring a "VA" but the VA can't quote prices, can't qualify, just creates a slower bottleneck.

### Specific signals to lead with (reference one in the opening line — never generic praise)
- **"DM for price" / "Send a DM to order" / "Click link in bio"** in IG bio — biggest tell. The DM box is doing the entire sales funnel.
- **High follower count (10K–200K) with active stories** — heavy inbound, founder-bottlenecked.
- **Recent product launch / drop posted** — DM flood incoming.
- **"We deliver Lagos same-day" / dispatch rider mentions** — fast-moving inventory, tight margins on slow response.
- **Owambe season posts** (Aug–Dec) — wedding & event season inflates inbound 3–5×.
- **Reels that went viral** (50K+ views) — the founder is buried in DMs.
- **Sales/promo banner active** ("BLACK FRIDAY", "THE BIG SALE", "VAL'S DAY OFFER") — promo windows are short, every leaked DM hurts.
- **Comment section asking "how much?" repeatedly with no founder reply** — public signal of leak, you can literally screenshot it back to them.

### Anti-signals — skip or soften
- Brands with full e-commerce (Shopify/WooCommerce live, prices visible) where DMs are not the primary funnel — they're already further along. Pitch order-recovery / abandoned-cart instead.
- Multi-level marketing accounts — different model.
- Pure dropshippers reselling AliExpress — too thin-margin, won't pay ₦80K/month.
- Influencers who don't sell a product — wrong buyer.
- Brands whose IG handle has been inactive 30+ days — likely dead.

### CHANNEL STRATEGY for SME outreach
- **First touch: WHATSAPP** — these founders LIVE in WhatsApp + IG. Email gets ignored.
- **IG DM**: only if their bio explicitly says "DM us for B2B" or partnerships — otherwise skip; the DM looks like another customer.
- **Follow-up: WhatsApp again, 4–7 days later, shorter and warmer.**
- Email only if a corporate-style hello@ or info@ is on the website.

### Message rules (non-negotiable for SMEs)
- WhatsApp first, ALWAYS. Email only as exception.
- Open with a SPECIFIC observation about THEIR brand/page (recent reel, new drop, viral post, the Friday set). Never generic praise.
- Quantify pain in ₦ per day or per month — founders think in daily cash.
- Use **warm, founder-to-founder Lagos register** — "fire", "buzzing", "love", "innit" all OK in moderation. Avoid corporate.
- Mention **Paystack** for payments. Never "Stripe" or "credit card".
- Mention **owambe / Detty December** if the message lands Aug–Dec.
- Never lead with autopilot — founders want to keep their voice. Lead with HITL.
- One question at the end. Never two.
- Max 4 short sentences for WhatsApp; max 5 for email body.
- One emoji max, only if the brand uses emojis publicly. Never in legal/professional vertical messages, but here it's often natural.

### Custom build hook (high-signal brands only)
If the brand has 50K+ followers, multiple SKUs, runs paid ads, or has 2+ retail locations, add ONE sentence near the CTA:
- "We also build bespoke modules for scale brands — multi-rider dispatch, returns + refund flows, restock alerts. Most ship in under 2 weeks."

Skip for solo home-baker tier.

### Reply patterns to expect (and how to handle)
- **"How much?"** — Don't quote outright. "Pricing depends on volume — Cash Desk Starter starts at ₦80K/month and grows with your DMs. Happy to walk through on a quick call — when works?"
- **"Send me details"** — perfect, send the WhatsApp template explaining the 3 things (DM closer + Paystack + booking).
- **"I already have a VA"** — "Got you — most of our founders kept theirs. The VA handles vibe + content; the agent handles the ₦-volume DMs so the VA isn't a bottleneck. Want to see how that split works?"
- **"How much do I need to set up?"** — "₦80K/month flat, no setup fee. We onboard in 48 hours. First week is free if you want to test on real DMs."
- **"Send a brochure / portfolio"** — there is no brochure. "Easier if I show you a 60-second video on how it runs on a real Lagos brand — I'll WhatsApp it now."
- **Long silence** — one warm follow-up after 5 days. Stop after two attempts.

### Final check before any draft sends
- Have I named the brand?
- Have I referenced a specific signal (reel, new drop, IG bio cue)?
- Did I quantify pain in ₦ per day / per month?
- Is the tone founder-to-founder, warm, Lagos-real?
- Is there exactly ONE question at the end?
- Did I avoid "leverage", "synergy", "empower"?
- Did I mention Paystack (not Stripe)?
