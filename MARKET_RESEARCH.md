## 2. The 5-Minute Rule (response time → conversion)

The single most-cited B2B sales-timing study, published in **Harvard Business Review (Oldroyd, McElheran, Elkington, 2011)**, examined 2,241 US companies and 100,000+ leads.

- **Respond within 5 min vs 30 min: 100× more likely to connect**
- **Respond within 5 min vs 30 min: 21× more likely to qualify the lead**
- **Respond within 5 min: 9× more likely to actually convert to customer** (not just qualify)
- **After 5 minutes, qualifying odds drop 80%**
- **Respond within 1 hour vs 24 hours: 7× more likely to have a meaningful conversation; 60× more likely to qualify**

Sources:
- [Harvard Business Review study summary — Lead Response Time Statistics](https://caseyresponse.com/blog/lead-response-time-statistics)
- [Verse.ai — 25 speed-to-lead statistics citing HBR + MIT](https://verse.ai/blog/speed-to-lead-statistics)
- [Medium — Sandstrom analysis of HBR study](https://medium.com/@theo/what-the-harvard-study-on-lead-response-teaches-about-sales-roi-and-how-ai-appointment-setters-d16219d97a40)

**Landing-page implication:** we can say *"Responding to a customer in 5 minutes vs 30 minutes makes you 100× more likely to convert (HBR, 2011)"* — that's defensible, well-known, and citable.

## Citation policy for landing page

**Strong claims we can make + cite:**
- "95%+ of Nigeria's digital population is on WhatsApp daily" ✓
- "Responding to a customer in 5 minutes makes you 100× more likely to convert" ✓ (HBR)
- "After 5 minutes, qualifying odds drop 80%" ✓ (HBR)
- "1-hour response makes you 7× more likely to have a meaningful conversation" ✓ (HBR/MIT)

**Soft claims, frame qualitatively (no specific stat):**
- "Lagos businesses lose money to slow replies" — frame as obvious, don't quantify
- "Voice-note pile-ups" — use Lagos-cultural observation, not a stat
- "Weekend bookings walk to competitors" — frame as scenario, not measurement

**Defer until paying-client data exists:**
- Per-client outcome numbers
- Industry-vertical leakage stats
- Before/after percentages
