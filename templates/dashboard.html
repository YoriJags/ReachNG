Unipile WhatsApp Setup — Do This For Every Client
Without this, all messages send from YOUR number. Each client must have their own.
1
Client gets a WhatsApp Business number — can be their existing business phone or a new SIM dedicated to outreach.
2
Go to Unipile dashboard → Accounts → Add Account → Select WhatsApp → client scans the QR code with their phone. Takes 30 seconds.
3
Copy the Account ID from Unipile (looks like acc_xxxxxxxx) — paste it into the client record below in the Unipile WhatsApp Account ID field.
4
All campaigns for this client now send from their number — not yours. Replies come back to their WhatsApp and ReachNG picks them up automatically.
⚠️ Cost: Unipile charges ~$10–20/month per connected WhatsApp account. Pass this to the client as part of their monthly plan. Never use your own number for client campaigns.
Client Onboarding SOP — From "Yes" to First Send
0They said yes — collect intake info — ask via WhatsApp: (1) business name & sector, (2) target customer description, (3) city / cities, (4) preferred tone (formal or casual), (5) which number to send from. This takes 5 minutes.
1Unipile QR scan — Client opens WhatsApp on their business phone → go to Unipile dashboard → Accounts → Add Account → WhatsApp → client scans QR. Takes 30 seconds. Copy the Account ID (looks like acc_xxxxxxxx) — you'll need it in Step 3.
2Set their plan & billing — choose Starter (₦50k/mo · 200 msgs), Growth (₦120k/mo · 500 msgs), or Agency (₦250k/mo · unlimited). Enter their monthly fee in the form. Payment status starts as Trial — flip to Active once they pay. Track in Control Tower tab.
3Create client record — fill all fields: name, vertical, city, plan, monthly fee, Unipile Account ID. Brief is the most important field — who they are, what they sell, their tone, their target customer. The more detail, the better the AI-generated messages.
4Generate portal link → send to client on WhatsApp. Say: "Here's your live dashboard — you can see every contact we reach out to, all replies, and your ROI in real time."
5Pick their suites — which of the 12 revenue products do they need? Invoice Chaser and Debt Collector are the default starters (every Lagos SME has overdue money on the street). Layer Fleet Dispatcher, School Fees, or MarketOS where it fits their business.
6Load their data — upload outstanding invoices, debt cases, trucks, or student roster via the suite tabs. The schedulers take over: invoice chase at 9am, debt collector at 9:30am, fleet escalation hourly, market alerts 3× daily. All drafts land in Message Queue for your tap-to-send.
7Month-end billing — Control Tower tab → find client → click Invoice → select month → Open Invoice → Print to PDF → send to client. Update payment status to Active once paid, Overdue if 7+ days late.
